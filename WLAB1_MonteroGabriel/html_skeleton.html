Myrtle Beach, South Carolina, USA

History

Before becoming a tourist attraction, the area used to be a fishing spot of the native american tribe know as the Waccamaw. In the 18th century, European settles would eventually move in and built houses and a plantationsystem that would extend to the ocean. Around 1900, a contest would be held to name the area, and was named after a local shrub (Myrica cerifera). In 1937, the Myrtle Beach Municiple Airport was built, allowing for more people to access the area. Since then the are a would become a popular tourist attraction for the general public, and became a revenue source to South Carolina.
